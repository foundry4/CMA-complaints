A business or person not following government guidance or laws on coronavirus, putting people at risk.

If you have seen something that you think goes against government guidance or the law and is likely to cause serious harm, you can report it. This could include businesses remaining open when they should be closed, or concerns about how employers are managing the risk of coronavirus with their staff or customers. Find out more about the latest government guidance and restrictions.

To report these issues, you should contact one or more of the following organisations:

Contact your local council

You should contact your local authority environmental health department about health and safety issues relating to some workplaces, including:

  • offices
  • shops
  • restaurants
  • pubs and clubs
  • leisure premises.

Find contact details for your local council

Health and Safety Executive

The Health and Safety Executive (HSE) is responsible for enforcing health and safety at workplaces, including:

  • factories
  • farms
  • building sites
  • mines
  • schools and colleges
  • fairgrounds
  • gas, electricity and water systems
  • hospitals and nursing homes
  • central and local government premises
  • offshore installations

Find out more and contact the Health and Safety Executive (HSE)

The Police

If you are reporting a person or business who has breached coronavirus measures, including:

  • not staying at home or self-isolating when required to do so
  • travelling in and out of different tiers or alert levels
  • not following restrictions on gatherings
  • a fraud or scam
  • an issue with face coverings not being worn

You should report this to the police.
Contact your local police force

If the behaviour you’d like to report has led to a business treating you unfairly (for example, in cancelling a product or service) it’s important you also report it to the CMA.
